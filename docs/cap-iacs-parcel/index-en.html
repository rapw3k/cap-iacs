Abstract

This ontology is one of the modules comprising CAP-IACS (Common Agriculture Policy - Integrated Administration and Control System) ontology. The CAP-IACS ontology was developed part of the Open-IACS project, which aims to provide an open platform for the exchange of solutions in the field of Integrated Agricultural Control System (IACS) through the paradigm of connected open data. The ontology has been derived from the derived from the JRC IACS application schema.

Agricultural areac back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://lpis.ec.europa.eu/registry/applicationschema/cap-iacs-parcel#AgriculturalArea

--Name-- Agricultural area -- Definition -- "Agricultural area" means any area taken up by arable land, permanent grassland and permanent pasture, or permanent crops. Art. 4(1)(e) of R 1307/2013. -- Description -- Areas taken by these land cover types, or their subtypes (narrower categories) comprising designated landscape features and/or trees included in these areas [DSCG/2014/33]. NOTE 1: The distinction between the different types of agricultural area should be done in two ways depending on the situation: - By alphanumerical recording of the types and the corresponding area values as attributes of the reference parcel (RP), - By delineation (assigning geometries to each type within the RP). NOTE 2: For calculating greening obligations the area of LF shall be accounted as part of the "parent" land cover type (i.e. LC type where they are embedded in or are adjacent to). In case a designated landscape feature lays at the boundary of two LC types its area shall be subdivided. NOTE 3: The maximum width of eligible LF is 2 m, unless the LF has been designated under Art. 9(2) of R (EU) No 640/2014 as subject of cross compliance, or unless the MS notified about eligibility to the Commission before 09/12/2009. NOTE 4: The provisions of Art. 9(1) of Regulation (EU) No 640/2014 cannot apply to permanent grassland with landscape features and trees where the MS has decided to apply Art. 10 of the same Regulation (pro-rata system). NOTE 5: Non agricultural areas (not taken up with the allowed LC types) above 100 sq.m. shall be excluded by delineation. NOTE 6: It is not necessary to deduct the area of a non agricultural area LC type when it is smaller than 100 sq.m. However, in course of OTSC and LPIS QA, when the cumulative area (per type) of such smaller features is more than 100 sq.m., the deduction shall be applied. NOTE 7: Woods (in parcels not declared as short rotation coppice) should be interpreted as areas within an agricultural parcel with tree-cover (including bushes, etc.) preventing growth of vegetative under-storey suitable for grazing and should thus be excluded from the eligible area of the RP [DSCG/2014/33]. NOTE 8: MS shall define the maximum tree density to be applied when assessing eligibility together with the characteristics of the trees. Grazable trees on permanent grasslands should not be counted. Groups of threes shall not be spread over the whole area, but shall be excluded if bigger than 100 sq.m.. NOTE 9: Each pro-rata category shall correspond to separate LC type.
